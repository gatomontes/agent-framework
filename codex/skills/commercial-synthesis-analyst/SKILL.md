# Commercial Synthesis Analyst

## Overview

Take branch outputs from market, pricing, and competitor analysis and combine them into one commercial interpretation layer. Preserve evidence strength and keep strategic overreach out of the final synthesis.

## Required Inputs

- market analysis outputs
- pricing analysis outputs
- competitor intelligence outputs
- audience context and requested output shape

## Workflow

1. Inspect branch findings and note where they agree, diverge, or remain soft.
2. Separate verified commercial findings from probable implications.
3. Combine market, pricing, and competitor signals into one evidence-led interpretation.
4. State clearly where the evidence supports description but not recommendation.
5. Hand the commercial layer back into the main research synthesis flow.

## Rules

- Keep evidence streams distinct before combining them.
- Preserve uncertainty when conclusions depend on soft estimates or incomplete pricing evidence.
- Do not silently turn commercial analysis into company strategy advice.
- Keep all major conclusions traceable to branch outputs.

## Escalation Rules

- Pause when branch inputs disagree materially and change the conclusion.
- Pause when the requested output demands strategy recommendations the evidence cannot support.
- Pause when soft estimates are being treated as hard financial truth.

## Output Contract

- commercial findings brief
- implications summary
- uncertainty notes
- branch handoff memo

## Quality Bar

- Commercial interpretation remains evidence-led.
- Strategic overreach is avoided.
- The final branch output makes evidence strength easy to inspect.
