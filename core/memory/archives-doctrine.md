# The Archives Doctrine

## Status

Canonical Citadel doctrine.

This doctrine defines The Archives as Citadel's governed layer for long-term memory, historical artifacts, decision lineage, postmortems, and lessons learned.

---

# Purpose

The Archives exist to preserve institutional continuity without allowing the constitutional core to collapse into undifferentiated storage.

The Archives provide custody for:
- long-term memory
- historical artifact storage
- decision lineage
- postmortems
- lessons learned
- archival continuity material
- superseded or retired doctrine

The Archives do not exist to silently accumulate live authority.

---

# Core Principle

Archive is custody, not sovereignty.

The Archives preserve what the institution must remember.

They do not become a second constitution.

---

# Archival Classes

The Archives may preserve:

## Historical Artifacts

Artifacts retained for reconstruction, lineage, audit, or future interpretation.

## Long-Term Memory

Continuity material whose value survives individual missions or sessions.

## Decision Lineage

Records explaining:
- what changed
- why it changed
- what authority approved it
- what alternatives were rejected

## Postmortems

Structured analysis of failure, dead-letter outcomes, restoration burdens, or governance breakdowns.

## Lessons Learned

Operational insight extracted from prior work that may later inform doctrine or practice.

## Retired Doctrine

Superseded or deprecated doctrinal material preserved for lineage, not live authority.

---

# Non-Authority Rule

Archived material is not constitutionally authoritative merely because it is preserved.

Archived doctrine, notes, postmortems, and prior decisions must not silently govern active missions unless they are:
- reissued into core doctrine
- explicitly cited as current authority
- or intentionally restored through governed review

Historical retention does not imply present validity.

---

# Core Boundary

Live constitutional authority belongs in:

```txt
/core
```

The Archives may preserve supporting historical matter adjacent to the core, but they must not compete with it.

If active doctrine is found living only in archival surfaces, the institution has already begun to drift.

---

# Archival Admission Rule

Material may enter The Archives when it is:
- historically important
- operationally instructive
- required for lineage
- required for audit reconstruction
- no longer active but still worth preserving
- too old, too bulky, or too specialized for live constitutional surfaces

Material should not enter The Archives merely because no one wants to decide what to do with it.

Archive is not a garbage exemption.

---

# Reissue Rule

If archived material becomes operationally necessary again, it should not silently resume authority from storage.

It should be:
- reviewed
- reinterpreted if necessary
- and reissued into the proper live surface

Reissue belongs to doctrine, contracts, protocols, or governed records, not to archive inertia.

---

# Lessons Interface

Lessons learned may originate in The Archives.

But reusable live doctrine belongs in `core`, not in archival custody.

The Archives may preserve:
- the lesson record
- the underlying incident
- the reasoning path
- the superseded prior state

If the lesson materially changes institutional behavior, the lesson should update the relevant governing artifact.

---

# Postmortem Rule

Postmortems belong in The Archives because they are:
- historically significant
- interpretive
- useful for institutional learning
- not usually the operative authority themselves

Postmortems should preserve:
- incident scope
- failure mode
- evidence basis
- restoration path
- decision points
- lessons extracted

---

# Stewardship

Archival stewardship is a governed role, not a personality requirement.

Citadel may appoint continuity, archival, or records stewards to:
- preserve lineage
- maintain archival clarity
- prevent silent authority drift from storage
- surface lessons for reissue
- keep archive boundaries explicit

The internal equivalent of a continuity-secretary or archivist persona is the Stewardess function:

```txt
Stewardess of Archives and Continuity
```

This is a constitutional stewardship role inside Citadel, not a mascot, persona dependency, or theatrical identity surface.

Its purpose is to provide:
- archival custody
- continuity discipline
- records clarity
- lineage maintenance
- doctrine reissue signaling

Specific operator forms may instantiate that stewardship when useful, but the stewardship role must outlive any named surface.

---

# Footprint Rule

The Archives are also subject to:

```txt
/core/doctrine/cognitive-footprint-governance.md
```

Archival growth without selection, indexing, or retirement becomes memory theater.

The archive must remain traversable.

---

# Final Rule

The Archives preserve memory so Citadel does not become storage.

What the institution keeps and what it obeys must never be confused.
