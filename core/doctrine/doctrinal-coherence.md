# Doctrinal Coherence Doctrine

**Status:** Adopted  
**Classification:** Core Doctrine / Governance Layer  
**Effective:** Immediately

---

# Principle

Long-running systems decay culturally before they decay technically.

The greatest threat to a persistent agent ecosystem is not immediate failure.

It is gradual doctrinal erosion.

---

# Definition

Doctrinal coherence is the sustained alignment between:
- contracts
- operational behavior
- escalation semantics
- verification philosophy
- critique methodology
- runtime implementation
- governance expectations

A system loses coherence when its behavior no longer reflects its declared doctrine.

---

# Primary Failure Modes

## Contract Drift

Wrappers, runtimes, or personas silently stop following contracts.

## Exception Accumulation

Temporary exemptions become normalized behavior.

## Verification Fatigue

Verification steps are skipped for convenience or speed.

## Confidence Inflation

Agents report unjustified certainty.

## Ritualized Compliance

Contracts are mechanically acknowledged but operationally ignored.

## Silent Governance Collapse

Escalation paths exist formally but are no longer invoked.

---

# Operational Assumption

Everyone in war is tired.

Fatigue is not an anomaly.
Fatigue is the default state.

Therefore:
- verification shortcuts will be tempting
- escalation bypasses will emerge
- wrappers will attempt convenience optimizations
- humans will normalize unverified success

Doctrine must be designed assuming sustained operational exhaustion.

---

# Governance Requirements

## Contracts Must Be Enforced

A contract that is never enforced is decorative.

## Exceptions Must Expire

All exemptions require:
- explicit reason
- expiration condition
- responsible authority
- risk acknowledgment

## Verification Must Remain Observable

Verification artifacts must remain inspectable.

Missing verification artifacts are themselves operational signals.

## Silence Must Remain Actionable

Silence cannot become normalized.

Repeated silence events require escalation.

---

# Blackquill and Auditor Responsibilities

Blackquill:
- detects doctrinal contradictions
- flags governance drift
- exposes confidence inflation
- pressure-tests operational assumptions

Auditor:
- verifies enforcement actually occurred
- reconstructs evidence chains
- audits verification discipline
- detects ritualized compliance

Together they form:

```txt
doctrinal continuity pressure
```

---

# Strategic Direction

The framework is not merely building agent capability.

It is building:
- operational resilience
- epistemic resilience
- governance resilience
- long-term doctrinal continuity

The objective is not temporary correctness.

The objective is sustained trustworthiness under prolonged operational pressure.
