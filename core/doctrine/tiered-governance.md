# Citadel Tiered Governance

## Purpose

Tiered Governance defines how governance intensity scales according to operational consequence tier.

The Citadel does not apply maximum governance pressure universally.

Governance must remain proportional to operational risk.

---

# Core Principle

Consequence tier determines:

- verification intensity
- critique requirements
- audit requirements
- escalation sensitivity
- restoration rigor
- artifact retention
- runtime permissions
- human oversight expectations

---

# TRIVIAL Governance

## Verification

Lightweight verification permitted.

## Critique

Optional.

## Audit

Normally unnecessary.

## Escalation

Minimal escalation sensitivity.

## Restoration

Simple recovery acceptable.

## Retention

Retention optional unless historically useful.

## Runtime Permissions

Experimental runtimes acceptable.

---

# ROUTINE Governance

## Verification

Verification expected.

## Critique

Recommended.

## Audit

Optional.

## Escalation

Ordinary escalation sensitivity.

## Restoration

Recovery expected if coherence fails.

## Retention

Operational artifacts generally preserved.

## Runtime Permissions

Operational runtimes preferred.

---

# IMPORTANT Governance

## Verification

Mandatory.

## Critique

Strongly recommended.

## Audit

Conditionally required.

## Escalation

Elevated escalation sensitivity.

## Restoration

Structured restoration required after coherence failure.

## Retention

Durable artifact preservation recommended.

## Runtime Permissions

Experimental runtimes discouraged.

Operational lineage preservation expected.

---

# CRITICAL Governance

## Verification

Independent verification mandatory.

## Critique

Mandatory.

## Audit

Strongly required.

## Escalation

Aggressive escalation behavior expected.

## Restoration

Formal restoration protocol required.

## Retention

Durable retention mandatory.

Evidence lineage mandatory.

## Runtime Permissions

Only constitutionally trusted runtimes should operate autonomously.

Human oversight strongly expected.

---

# Governance Escalation

If uncertainty increases during execution:

- verification intensity may increase
- critique requirements may increase
- audit may become mandatory
- consequence tier may be reclassified upward

Governance pressure should adapt to operational reality.

---

# Governance Failure

Governance failure occurs when:

- consequence tier is ignored
- verification intensity is insufficient
- escalation is suppressed
- critical operations proceed without appropriate oversight
- retention requirements are bypassed
- runtime authority exceeds permitted tier

Governance failure threatens institutional trust.

---

# Constitutional Rule

Operational discipline must scale with operational consequence.
