# Capability Bundle Governance

## Status

Canonical AFW doctrine.

This doctrine defines how reusable capabilities may carry supporting references, scripts, generated artifacts, and assets without collapsing governance into opaque local machinery.

---

# Purpose

This doctrine exists to prevent:
- useful capability bundles being lost because only the primary skill document was treated as real
- hidden authority inside helper scripts or reference shelves
- generated support artifacts being mistaken for source of truth
- assets and utilities drifting into doctrine by accident

---

# Core Principle

A reusable capability may be a bundle.

The bundle must remain inspectable, governable, and subordinate to Citadel law.

---

# Bundle Components

A capability bundle may include:
- a primary capability artifact
- supporting references
- helper scripts
- generated support artifacts
- bundled assets

These components do not share equal constitutional weight.

## Primary Capability Artifact

Defines the capability's purpose, triggers, inputs, outputs, escalation path, and operational boundaries.

## Supporting References

Preserve templates, heuristics, mappings, examples, or playbooks that help the capability operate consistently.

References may guide execution.

They do not become governing law merely by being nearby.

## Helper Scripts

Perform repeatable bounded operations that are better executed concretely than improvised in prose.

Scripts must remain explicit dependencies of the capability rather than hidden implementation assumptions.

## Generated Support Artifacts

May summarize a larger source or produce inventory-like working surfaces.

Generated artifacts are acceptable when:
- provenance is clear
- regeneration path is explicit
- they are not mistaken for source of truth

## Bundled Assets

May include templates, static assets, or other support files needed for repeatable output.

Assets support capability behavior.

They do not define doctrine.

---

# Registration Expectations

When a capability bundle is registered, it should preserve enough information to answer:
- what the primary capability artifact is
- what supporting references it expects
- what scripts it depends on
- what generated artifacts it may emit or consume
- what assets are required
- what authority assumptions the bundle carries
- what verification path applies

---

# Script Rule

If a capability depends on a script, the dependency should be explicit in the capability contract or registration record.

Script-backed capability is acceptable.

Hidden script dependency is not.

---

# Reference Rule

References may preserve execution-relevant structure.

They should be compressed toward:
- triggers
- behavior
- outputs
- escalation

Presentation-only bulk should not be preserved as constitutional material.

---

# Source-Of-Truth Rule

Generated support artifacts, examples, and references must not silently replace the primary source of truth.

If they disagree with governing doctrine or the primary capability artifact, doctrine wins.

---

# Final Rule

Capability bundles are real operational artifacts.

They are not exempt from inspection just because part of the bundle is a script, reference shelf, or asset folder.
