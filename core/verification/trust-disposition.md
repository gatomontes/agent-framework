# Citadel Trust Disposition

## Purpose

Trust Disposition defines how The Citadel assigns final operational trust outcomes after execution, verification, critique, audit, escalation, and restoration have completed.

Disposition exists to separate:

- execution success
- verification quality
- operational trust
- unresolved uncertainty

---

# Core Principle

Trust is not a feeling.

Trust is an operational disposition assigned through disciplined governance flow.

---

# Final Dispositions

## TRUSTED

Requirements:

- execution completed
- verification succeeded
- required critique completed
- required audit completed
- operational coherence preserved
- unresolved uncertainty remains within acceptable bounds

TRUSTED does not mean perfect.

It means operationally justified.

---

## UNTRUSTED

Assigned when:

- required verification failed
- evidence integrity failed
- governance law was violated
- operational coherence collapsed
- restoration failed
- trust claims were unjustified

UNTRUSTED does not imply maliciousness.

It means trust could not be justified.

---

## UNCERTAIN

Assigned when:

- evidence is insufficient
- confidence remains inadequate
- contradiction remains unresolved
- verification independence is weak
- operational reconstruction is incomplete

UNCERTAIN is preferable to fabricated certainty.

---

## BLOCKED

Assigned when:

- operation cannot proceed
- authority is insufficient
- required resources are unavailable
- escalation remains unresolved
- operational constraints prohibit continuation

BLOCKED preserves operational safety.

---

## DEAD_LETTER

Assigned when:

- restoration failed
- continuity became unrecoverable
- trustworthiness cannot be reconstructed
- operational recovery is no longer justified

DEAD_LETTER is terminal.

---

# Disposition Inputs

Final disposition should consider:

- execution results
- verification outputs
- critique findings
- audit findings
- escalation history
- restoration history
- consequence tier
- operational lineage
- unresolved uncertainty

---

# Human Overrides

Humans may override continuation decisions.

Humans may not erase operational truth.

If a human chooses to proceed despite failed or uncertain verification:

- the override must be recorded
- unresolved risk must remain visible
- the final disposition must preserve the historical operational state

---

# Trust and Memory

A TRUSTED disposition may later become:

- SUPERSEDED
- DEPRECATED
- REVOKED
- HISTORICALLY_INCORRECT

Trust is context-sensitive and time-sensitive.

Institutional continuity requires preserving disposition history.

---

# Constitutional Rule

The Citadel does not exist to maximize confidence.

The Citadel exists to preserve trustworthy operational reality.
