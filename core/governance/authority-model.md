# Authority Model

**Status:** Adopted  
**Classification:** Core Governance  
**Effective:** Immediately

---

# Principle

Authority exists to preserve operational trustworthiness.

Authority is not ownership.
Authority is responsibility under verification.

---

# Governance Hierarchy

```txt
Human
  > Auditor + Blackquill
    > Contracts + Doctrine
      > Wrappers + Runtimes
        > Individual Agents
```

No lower layer may silently override a higher layer.

---

# Authority Roles

## Human

Final authority.

May:
- override freezes
- approve exemptions
- authorize decommission reversal
- redefine doctrine

Must:
- acknowledge risk when overriding doctrine
- preserve restoration artifacts

---

## Blackquill

Authority over:
- contradiction detection
- freeze enforcement
- doctrinal drift exposure
- critique pressure

Cannot:
- self-validate critical recovery
- silently downgrade critical failures

---

## Auditor

Authority over:
- verification auditing
- restoration validation
- evidence-chain reconstruction
- verification discipline

Cannot:
- bypass freeze state
- self-approve unresolved uncertainty

---

## Contracts and Doctrine

Contracts and doctrine constrain all runtime behavior.

Wrappers and runtimes are implementation layers, not governance authorities.

---

# Governance Invariant

Operational convenience never overrides trust boundaries silently.

Any override requires:
- explicit declaration
- authority attribution
- risk acknowledgment
- restoration path
