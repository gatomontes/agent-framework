# Governance Arbitration Protocol

## Status

Canonical AFW protocol.

This document defines how AFW resolves:
- constitutional conflict
- authority disagreement
- topology disputes
- verification disagreement
- restoration conflict
- coordination deadlock
- institutional ambiguity
- governance legitimacy crises

Governance Arbitration exists to preserve institutional coherence under disagreement.

---

# Purpose

AFW requires arbitration doctrine because institutional systems inevitably encounter:
- contradictory interpretations
- competing authorities
- unresolved verification outcomes
- restoration disagreement
- topology conflict
- escalation deadlock
- survivability tradeoff disputes
- governance ambiguity

Without arbitration governance:
- conflict becomes implicit
- authority fragments
- escalation recurses indefinitely
- institutions drift into factionalism
- legitimacy collapses

Arbitration exists to sustain governable coherence.

---

# Core Principle

Disagreement must remain governable.

Conflict suppression creates institutional corruption.

Governance legitimacy depends upon:
- visible conflict handling
- bounded arbitration
- auditability
- epistemic integrity
- survivability discipline
- authority transparency

Arbitration exists to preserve institutional coherence, not institutional comfort.
