- coordination semantics
- authority interaction
- concurrent topology ownership
- arbitration escalation
- dependency synchronization
- split-brain prevention
- coordination survivability
- deadlock handling

Operator coordination exists to preserve institutional coherence under distributed operational reasoning.

# Purpose

AFW requires explicit coordination doctrine because multiple operators create risks including:
- authority collision
- coordination fragmentation
- recursive escalation
- topology divergence
- split-brain orchestration
- verification conflict
- dependency instability
- governance deadlock

Without coordination governance:
- operators compete implicitly
- topology becomes unstable
- accountability dissolves
- restoration becomes chaotic
- institutional coherence collapses
