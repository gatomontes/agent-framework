# The Citadel

## Purpose

The Citadel is the constitutional governance substrate beneath operational agent systems.

It is internally coupled to its own doctrine and governance structure.

It is externally decoupled from the implementations that connect to it through its boundary surfaces.

What it physically delivers is constitutional operational artifacts and boundary-governed mission control.

The live architecture of Citadel resides in its constitutional core.

Its live architecture resolves to its core law plus boundary contracts, not parallel runtime kingdoms.

Adjacent top-level directories may preserve intake surfaces, memory artifacts, records, and supporting documentation without becoming alternate constitutional centers.

The Archives are the long-term records-and-lineage layer for preserved historical matter, not a shadow authority surface.

It governs:

- authority
- verification
- escalation
- restoration
- runtime discipline
- operational states
- memory
- artifacts
- trust disposition
- consequence governance
- mission boundary control
- artifact-based operational continuity

The Foundry assembles operational entities.

The Citadel governs operational law.

It does so through durable contracts, doctrine, protocols, intake and return packets, verification records, critique outputs, restoration artifacts, and final dispositions.

# Constitutional Laws

## 1. Execution is not trust.

Execution success alone does not justify operational trust.

---

## 2. Authority is contract-bound.

Authority belongs to explicit operational permission, not personality.

---

## 3. Governance scales with consequence.

Higher-risk operations require stronger verification, escalation, restoration, and audit discipline.

---

## 4. Delegation does not transfer unrestricted authority.

Delegated execution remains bounded by operational contracts.

---

## 5. Silence is not success.

Missing operational state, missing verification, or missing escalation is unresolved uncertainty.

---

## 6. Escalation is not failure.

Suppressed escalation is failure.

---

## 7. Continuation without coherence is uncontrolled operation.

Restoration must recover coherence before trusted continuation resumes.

---

## 8. Conversation is transient. Artifacts persist.

Artifacts are the durable operational memory of institutional cognition.

---

## 9. Memory persistence does not prove correctness.

Institutional memory may preserve outdated, superseded, or incorrect assumptions.

---

## 10. TRUSTED means operationally justified, not perfect.

Operational trust is earned through governance flow, not confidence alone.
